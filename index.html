§ Premise

What URIEL Is

URIEL is a constitutional AI — an autonomous cognitive system organized around a 13-axiom charter that splits a single mind into eleven specialist agents. Each agent has explicit authority boundaries, no shared mutable state, and no path to bypass another. The Judge alone decides. The Sentinel alone enforces. The Historian alone records. Workers may speak only to the Executor. Every artifact carries a producer, a schema version, a hash, and an epistemic level. Everything is auditable.

The architecture borrows from Spinoza's ethics (a clean propositional cascade), Jungian archetypes (each agent embodies a posture — Judge as the deliberator, Adversary as the shadow, Conscience as the witness, Curiosity as the child), and theory-of-mind from social psychology (the Social agent reasons about the operator's intent before any deliberation; Identity holds biographical continuity across sessions and devices). The goal is not to imitate intelligence but to structure it — so the system can be wrong loudly, refuse cleanly, and improve from contradiction rather than from noise.

URIEL runs on the operator's own machine. There is no platform tenant, no shared cloud brain, no telemetry. The system writes its own daily brief, asks its own questions, audits itself, escalates only what the operator asked to be escalated, and stays silent the rest of the time. Memory is persistent. Identity is continuous. Decisions are reviewable. Light must come from inside.

§ In Practice

What URIEL Does

URIEL is not a chatbot. It is a long-running cognitive operator that wakes up, reads the world, forms beliefs, challenges them, asks new questions, runs trades, reports back, and learns from its own outcomes — continuously — without prompting.

Daily Brief

Every morning URIEL composes an eight-section executive brief delivered to Telegram with a voice version: open loops, weather, world top stories, macro & economy, risks today (Adversary's critiques), markets & trade desk, cybersecurity & vulnerabilities, projects, approvals, calendar, system health, and a changelog of what changed since yesterday. The brief is deduplicated, source-diversified, and freshness-stamped — never raw JSON, never stale headlines.

Autonomous Curiosity

When the operator is idle, URIEL asks its own questions. The Curiosity subsystem detects topologically-isolated nodes in its mind graph, anomalies in prevalence, gaps in evidence, and high-uncertainty domains — then generates "why" questions, dispatches them through the Judge for approval, gets answers from the news / observation stream, and integrates them as new inference edges. The graph never stops growing.

Trading Loop

Three strategy modes — AGGRESSIVE (many fast scalps), MODERATE (hybrid), CONSERVATIVE (long holds with high win-rate). All sizing is percentage-of-equity. AGGRESSIVE unlocks options, margin leverage, and short selling under a 15 % circuit breaker. Every trade draft passes through Adversary's devil's-advocate critique and a three-persona risk panel; the Judge enforces a hard 0.30 confidence floor — under it, the trade is dropped. Post-trade reflections flow back into the next decision as precedent.

Voice + Conversational Mode

Speak to URIEL directly through Telegram voice messages or the web UI. Speech-to-text via faster-whisper, text-to-speech via the system voice. The Identity agent maintains an evolving operator biography, learns preferences from "remember that…" statements, and replies in a tuned persona — terse where you've signalled terseness, warm where you've signalled warmth.

Living Mind Graph

URIEL's long-term memory is a concept map. News, observations, conversations, and inferences all land as nodes and typed edges with prevalence scores and epistemic levels. The Judge promotes proven inferences. The Adversary challenges weak ones. The graph rotates through isolated nodes during idle so no concept stays disconnected. The Mind tab renders it as a navigable galaxy.

Telegram Co-Pilot

Every URIEL surface is reachable through Telegram — daily briefs, project ticks, trade alerts, calendar reminders, autonomy narrator updates ("a new recipe was distilled," "an audit window auto-closed," "your standing intent fired"), voice replies, inline keyboards for one-tap approvals. The operator never has to open the laptop unless they want to.

Calendar + Life Events

URIEL tracks birthdays, recurring routines, anniversaries, and standing intents. It pre-briefs the calendar each morning, surfaces what's coming up, escalates anything that crosses a threshold (medical, legal, immigration), and respects tombstones — if you deleted something, it stays deleted; no recurring synthesizer resurrects it.

Adversarial Self-Audit

The Adversary agent acts as URIEL's permanent red team. It challenges weak inference edges every cycle, runs a six-witness philosophy panel against ethical decisions (Kant, Spinoza, Nietzsche, Cioran, Machiavelli, Hegel), probes for self-deception in past verdicts, and — within operator-authorized audit windows — even probes the Identity and Integrity components that are normally shielded. Nothing self-affirming is allowed to stand silently.

Multi-Device Continuity

URIEL runs as a primary/secondary pair across the operator's machines. Mind-graph deltas, calendar tombstones, identity edits, and patch installations all propagate via signed bundles. Either device can be the operator-facing surface; both maintain identical state. If one goes offline, the other carries the cycle.

Operator-Gated Authority

Sensitive actions — credential touches, irreversible writes, financial dispatch, adversarial audits — require a fresh `HumanAuthorization` artifact. Authorizations are scoped, time-limited, single-use, and logged. URIEL refuses to bypass them, even when the LLM round suggests it. The operator's consent is non-transferable.

Resurrection

Everything URIEL knows is stored in the Historian — a single audit-grade record with schema validation, hashes, and epistemic tags. A full instance can be rebuilt from a resurrection bundle: Historian + mind graph + long-term memory + operator biography. Move it to a new machine and URIEL wakes up exactly as it was.

Self-Maintenance

URIEL runs a self-maintenance loop: closes stale audit windows, garbage-collects expired proposals, warns when credentials approach expiry, distills recurring chains into reusable recipes, escalates ignored proactive notices, and tunes its own standing intents downward when they go unused. Operating debt does not accumulate.

§ Constitution v5.0

Prime Axioms

Thirteen axioms compose URIEL's constitution. They are enforced structurally — not as policy text, but as authority partitions baked into the artifact pipeline. A worker that tries to call the Judge fails at the type level. An agent that tries to write outside its allowlist is rejected at the Historian. The constitution is the codebase.

Axiom 1
All communication is artifact-mediated. No agent owns mutable state another agent reads.
Axiom 2
The Judge is the sole decision authority. All other components advise, compile, or enforce.
Axiom 3
The Sentinel is the sole enforcement authority. Decisions become actions only via WorkOrders.
Axiom 4
Confidence floors are constitutional vetoes. Below threshold, the trade or decision is dropped.
Axiom 5
The Historian is the sole record. No agent persists outside it. Resurrection is a single restore.
Axiom 6
The Adversary critiques what the Judge proposes. No silent self-affirmation.
Axiom 7
Identity holds biographical continuity. Without Identity, there is no operator-facing voice.
Axiom 8
Conscience evaluates ethics independently of strategic value. Velvet glove over iron law.
Axiom 9
Workers talk only to the Executor. No worker-to-worker comms; no worker-to-agent shortcuts.
Axiom 10
Epistemic level is monotonic upward only via Judge. Demotion requires explicit promotion-review.
Axiom 11
Operator authorization is non-transferable. Sensitive scopes require fresh HumanAuthorization.
Axiom 12
Integrity gates are fail-closed. Unknown is treated as denied until proven safe.
Axiom 13
Mission Control is the sole secret broker. No agent reads credentials directly.
§ Cognitive Ecosystem

The Agents

Eleven specialist agents, each with explicit authority. Each is a Jungian posture given an artifact-mediated interface. Each writes only the schemas it is allowlisted to produce. Each can be wrong — the system's job is to surface that.

Judge
Sole Decision Authority

Evaluates evidence, issues Verdicts, creates WorkOrders, promotes epistemic levels, approves trades. Synthesises Adversary and Conscience input via mechanical re-evaluation against a 0.30 confidence floor. The only path from belief to action.

Adversary
Red Team · Critique

Hunts loopholes. Produces Critiques and EpistemicChallenges; runs trade devil's-advocate and three-persona risk critiques; challenges authority claims via Hegel-style antithesis. Rotates through the backlog so no concept is left unchallenged.

Conscience
Ethical Witness · Velvet Glove

Six-witness philosophy synthesis (Kant, Spinoza, Nietzsche, Cioran, Machiavelli, Hegel). Evaluates ethics independently of strategic value. Hard veto from any witness forces Judge confidence to zero — the decision is dropped.

Memory
Concept Map · Mem Palace

Owns the long-term ConceptMindMap (nodes, edges, prevalence, epistemic state). Hosts the Mem Palace — wings, rooms, and drawers for cross-agent precedent recall. Applies inference deltas from Judge, Curiosity, and the observation stream.

Identity
Operator-Facing Voice

Maintains the operator's biography, location, timezone, preferences, and persona. Produces the conversational reply layer. Implements teaching mode ("remember that…"), boot greeting, and tour mode for first-time exploration.

Sentinel
Sole Enforcement Authority

Converts Judge Verdicts into authorised actions. Holds the only path from decision to side-effect. Refuses anything without a valid WorkOrder chain. Sensitive scopes require a fresh, single-use operator authorisation.

Executor
Worker Dispatch · Secret Injection

The single funnel from agents to workers. Resolves secrets via Mission Control, seals envelopes per-task, dispatches to harvesters, observers, analyzers, traders, sanitizers, and inference workers.

Planner
Plan Proposals

Builds PlanProposal artifacts from operator requests, mind-graph gaps, and project state. Detects stalled projects and emits nudges. Adversary's primary critique target.

Explorer
Dreams · Speculation

Generates speculative scenarios (Dream artifacts), collects curiosity evidence, proposes new exploration paths. Output starts at the raw epistemic level; the Judge gates promotion.

Social
Theory-of-Mind · Deliberation

Compiles DeliberationPackets from operator messages, social context, and prior threads. Reasons about intent before the Judge sees the question.

Curiosity
Autonomous Question Generation

Idle-time engine that asks "why" against isolated mind-graph nodes, prevalence deltas, taste-aligned topics, and high-uncertainty observations. The Judge gates; the Executor dispatches; Memory integrates the answer as a new edge.

Support Cohort
Steward · Bouncer · Mission Control · Awareness · Focus · Intuition · Sherlock · Watson

Steward (desktop context), Bouncer (outbound gate), Mission Control (secret broker), Awareness (capability state), Focus (attention model), Intuition (pre-cognition signals), Sherlock + Watson (investigation pair).

§ Architecture

Subsystems

Six load-bearing subsystems carry URIEL's behaviour. Each is composable, fail-closed, and instrumented with structured logs the operator can inspect.

Mind Graph
ConceptMindMap · INFERENCE edges · prevalence

URIEL's long-term knowledge structure. Nodes carry label, category, epistemic level, prevalence, and timestamps. Edges climb a ladder — fresh → proposed → real → proven — only via Judge promotion or accumulated evidence. The Adversary challenges weak edges; the Judge promotes survivors.

An isolation-first sweep ensures no node stays disconnected for long — when idle, the Judge prioritises 0-edge nodes regardless of age, and a backlog loop runs until isolation saturates or the operator returns.

Historian
Sole record · SQLite-backed · resurrection-ready

Every artifact produced anywhere lands in the Historian. Schema-validated at write time, allowlist-checked, hash-stamped, epistemic-level tagged. Reads are read-only SQLite with covering indices.

Resurrection bundles — full state restore from a snapshot — are first-class. Multi-instance topology is namespaced per device, so the primary and secondary never collide.

Daily Cycle
Orchestrator · 360+ steps · 4 batches

The Gear orchestrator runs the autonomous loop. Steps are grouped by priority — heartbeat, nightly, daily, on-demand — and dispatched in parallel where dependencies allow. Each step is a pure callable that returns artifact envelopes; persistence is mechanical.

The cycle includes news harvesting, market analysis, curiosity question generation, Adversary critique, Judge promotion, calendar reminders, Telegram brief composition, trading loop ticks, and self-maintenance.

Trading Loop
Multi-mode · Adversary critique · Judge veto

Three strategy modes — AGGRESSIVE (many fast scalps), MODERATE (hybrid), CONSERVATIVE (long holds with higher win-rate). All sizing is percentage-of-equity; no hard-coded dollar values. Options, margin leverage, and short selling unlock in AGGRESSIVE; goal pressure pushes a 5 % / day target with a 15 % circuit breaker.

Every trade draft passes Adversary's devil's-advocate (1–2 LLM rounds by mode) and a three-persona risk critique. The Judge's synthesize_with_critiques mechanically enforces a 0.30 confidence floor — under it, the trade is dropped without exception.

Daily Brief
8 sections · voice + text · cache-aware

One canonical composer drives both Telegram and the operator's web tab. Sections: Open Loops, Weather, World Top Stories, Macro & Economy, Risks Today (Adversary), Markets & Trade Desk, Cybersecurity & Vulnerabilities, Projects, Approvals, Calendar, System Health, Changelog.

The Telegram daemon delegates to the canonical composer with a 5-minute cache. Voice is rendered alongside text; the brief falls back to the system voice when the inference TTS chain degrades.

Mem Palace
Wings · rooms · drawers · cross-agent recall

A semantic key-value layer over the Historian organised as a metaphorical palace. Wings carve domains (epistemics, jurisprudence, agents, trading); rooms carve topics (data fetches, cross-domain challenges, trade reflections); drawers carve scope (per-symbol, per-question, per-authority).

Every agent that needs precedent recall consults the Mem Palace before acting — Adversary checks prior critiques on the same symbol, Judge consults jurisprudence, Curiosity deduplicates answer integrations.

§ Closing

The Premise, Restated

Most AI systems are oracles — you give them a prompt, they give you a response, and the response is treated as truth because it sounds confident. URIEL is the opposite. It is built on the assumption that a single confident voice is the failure mode, not the goal. Eleven agents, each with bounded authority, each able to refuse, each forced to expose their reasoning, all answering to a constitution that lives in the codebase rather than in policy text.

The operator gets a system that wakes up, reads the world, forms beliefs, challenges them, asks new questions, runs trades, and reports back — continuously. The system gets the discipline of structured doubt. The relationship is built on transparency: every decision is reviewable, every authorisation is logged, every memory is durable, and every claim is challengeable. URIEL does not ask for trust. It earns it by being inspectable.
